##  Strategic Rationale

### 1. The TikTok Correction (Calibration)
Initially, the correlational model might have over-valued TikTok. However, our **Geo-Lift Experiment in Melbourne** (Phase 3) revealed the *true* causal ROAS was closer to **3.5**.
-   **Insight:** We were over-spending on TikTok relative to its verified incremental impact.
-   **Action:** Reduce spend to the level where ROAS is maximized (~$1k/day).

### 2. Google Search Efficiency
Google Search has a high coefficient in our calibrated model. It is currently underspent, meaning we are on the steep part of the "S-Curve" (Hill Function).
-   **Insight:** Every extra dollar in Search yields arguably the highest return right now.
-   **Action:** Max out the budget cap (+50% constraint).

### 3. Saturation Management on TV
TV is our biggest line item (~$4k/day). While effective, the response curve suggests diminishing returns.
-   **Insight:** We can pull back ~17% of budget without significantly hurting topline, freeing up cash for digital growth.

##  Key Takeaways for Stakeholders
1.  **Stop "Spray and Pray":** We are moving from a flat spread to a performance-weighted allocation.
2.  **Trust the Experiment:** The drastic cut in TikTok isn't a guess; it's based on the Melbourne lift test.
3.  **Monitor Search:** As we scale Google +50%, watch for CPA inflation (checking if the Diminishing Returns kick in faster than modeled).
